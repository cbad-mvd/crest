The CREST Experience

The purpose of CREST Education Center, Inc. is to provide special education and therapies to students whose needs cannot be, or are not being, met by their local school district. CREST is a licensed and accredited school and therapy center for students ages 4 years to 22 years who present with mild, moderate or severe disabilities, including those who fall within the definition of Autism Spectrum Disorder. These disabilities are demonstrated by delays in several developmental areas, including speech, language, learning, behavior and fine/gross motor skills. The educational environment is designed to provide intensive, individualized instruction in all academic areas, making use of strengths while remediating areas of need. Individual or small group therapies in the areas of speech, language, sensory processing, behavior and motor skills focus on further individualizing and intensifying studentsâ€™ educational experience. The end result is students who have maximized their potential and are either able to return to a less restrictive environment (their local school) or become a productive member of society.

Our Story

Children Matter

CREST carries on a tradition and successful methodology created by two pioneers in the field of special education. Over 60 years ago, long before the term autism was widely used, Aleen Agranowitz and Gladys Gleason started working with children who had speech and language difficulties. The founders of CREST spent over 50 years working side by side with Aleen and Gladys in developing strategies to best meet the unique needs of their students. CREST continues to embody the legacy of that work.
